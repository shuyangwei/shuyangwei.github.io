excerpt: 'This study is based on survey data collected from children aged 7 to 15 in three cities of Jiangsu Province, Nanjing, Wuxi, and Yancheng, collected in 2019. It analyzes the acceptability, behavioral patterns, and psychological patterns of the first children in families that have had a second child (also called two-children families), and compares these characteristics with those of the children in only-child families. The aim of the research is to explore the adaptation status and issues faced by the first child in two-children families. The findings reveal that the first children in two-children families have a higher acceptability to the second child than that of the children in only-children families. However, as age increases, the acceptability tends to decrease. After the birth of the second child, the first child often establishes a good playmate-like relationship with the second child, and their behavioral patterns generally undergo positive changes. Nevertheless, the lack of communication between parents and the first child leads to a more passive acceptability of the second child.'
