excerpt: 'This study is based on survey data collected from children aged 7 to 15 in three cities of Jiangsu Province, Nanjing, Wuxi, and Yancheng, collected in 2019. It analyzes the acceptance level, behavioral patterns, and psychological patterns of the first child in families that have had a second child, and compares these characteristics with those of the only-child families that have not yet had a second child. The aim of research is to explore the adaptation status and issues faced by two-children families. The findings reveal that compared to only children, the first child in two-children families generally exhibits a higher level of acceptance to the second child. However, as age increases, the acceptance level tends to decrease. After the second child's birth, the first child often establishes a good playmate-like relationship with the second child, and their behavioral patterns generally undergo positive changes. Nevertheless, the lack of communication between parents and the first child leads to a more passive acceptance process of the second child.'
