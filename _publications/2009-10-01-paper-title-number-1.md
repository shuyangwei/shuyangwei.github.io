excerpt: 'This study is based on survey data from children aged 7 to 15 in three cities of Jiangsu Province, Nanjing, Wuxi, and Yancheng, collected in 2019. It analyzes the acceptance level, behavioral pattern characteristics, and psychological pattern characteristics of the first child in families that have had a second child, and compares these characteristics with the traits of only children in families that have not yet had a second child. The aim is to explore the current state of adaptation and issues faced by the first child in families with two children. The findings reveal that compared to only children, the first child in families with a second child generally exhibits a higher level of acceptance towards the second child. However, as age increases, the acceptance level of children towards a second child tends to decrease. After the birth of the second child, the first child often establishes a good playmate-like relationship with the second child, and their behavioral patterns generally undergo positive changes. Nevertheless, there is a situation where the lack of communication between parents and the first child leads to a more passive acceptance process of the second child.'
