About

My hometown is Yongjia, Wenzhou, China, a place known for its rivers, mountains, opera, ancient villages, wheat cakes, businessmen, and mathematicians. I love speaking the Yongjia Wu Chinese, my mother tongue, with my parents wherever we go. It feels like a private language that only we can understand.

Before being a member of Spartans, I roamed around linguistics at Nanjing (2018-2022), Hangzhou (2022-2025), and Manchester (2023.9-2024.1).

Besides linguistics, I enjoy painting, playing the piano (my piano instructor is Hajin Kim), cooking, anaerobic exercise, reading, writing, collecting quarters, knitting, calligraphy, hiking, and birding.

Ongoing Projects

Prosodic inference in Mandarin wh-in-situ. How do listeners distinguish wh-questions from wh-declaratives when segmental strings are identical? I test how different prosodic cues are weighted and where inference breaks down.
