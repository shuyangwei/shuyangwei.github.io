Wèi, Shūyáng
[weɪ⁵¹ ʂu⁵⁵ jɑŋ³⁵]
In Mandarin Chinese, Shuyang literally means clean water and sunshine.

About

I am a first-year PhD student in Linguistics at Michigan State University. My research interests include semantics, pragmatics, the syntax–semantics interface, experimental methods, and computational modeling.

Before MSU, I received an MA in Linguistics and Applied Linguistics at Zhejiang University, and a BA in Teaching Chinese to Speakers of Other Languages at Nanjing University.

Now

I am currently developing projects on Mandarin wh-in-situ constructions and how listeners infer clause type from prosody when morphosyntactic cues are limited.
