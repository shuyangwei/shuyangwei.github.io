Wèi, Shūyáng
[weɪ⁵¹ ʂu⁵⁵ jɑŋ³⁵]
In Mandarin Chinese, Shuyang evokes meanings associated with clean water and mild sunshine.

Home

Welcome to my homepage! I am a first-year PhD student (2025 Cohort) in Linguistics at Michigan State University. My research interests include phonetics, phonology, syntax-phonology interface, syntax–semantics interface, experimental methods, and computational modeling. My advisors are Karthik Durvasula, Brian Buccola, and Alan Hezao Ke. I am a part of Semantics and Pragmatics Lab, MSU PhonoGroup, and Minimalism, Learning and Computational Psycholinguistics Lab.

News

I am currently developing projects on Mandarin wh-in-situ constructions and how listeners infer clause type from prosody when morphosyntactic cues are limited.
