Wèi, Shūyáng
[weɪ⁵¹ ʂu⁵⁵ jɑŋ³⁵]
In Mandarin Chinese, Shuyang literally means clean water and sunshine.

About

I am a first-year PhD student in Linguistics at Michigan State University. My research interests include phonetics, phonology, syntax–semantics interface, experimental methods, and computational modeling.

Before being a member of Spartans, I received an MA in Linguistics and Applied Linguistics at Zhejiang University, and a BA in Teaching Chinese to Speakers of Other Languages at Nanjing University.

Besides linguistics, I love painting, playing the piano (my piano instructor is Dr. Hyemin Kim), knitting, reading, writing, cooking, calligraphy, anaerobic exercise, hiking, and birding.

News

I am currently developing projects on Mandarin wh-in-situ constructions and how listeners infer clause type from prosody when morphosyntactic cues are limited.
